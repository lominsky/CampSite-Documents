Dear First name,

This letter confirms your employment at Shire Village (hereinafter referred to as "Employer") for the camping season subject to the terms and conditions listed below:

  1. Arrival at camp on 2017 > Start date, to stay through 2017 > End date.
  2. Employees are responsible for traveling to camp. Reimbursement will be provided for a bus ticket from New York City to Northampton, MA (if receipt is provided). The employee must provide own transportation from camp.
  3. Staff members MUST submit a medical report, dated within the last 12 months and including tuberculin test to the director (address posted above) no later than June 15th as a protective health measure. Staff members who were employed by Shire Village Camp during the summer of 2015 and who provided the camp with a full medical report for that summer do not need to submit a form. Please note that staff members who have worked as employees at Shire Village Camp for more than 2 consecutive summers must submit a new and complete medical report every other summer. Staff members who do not have the required medical form will not be allowed to begin work. Only staff members hired 3 weeks or less before the start of work may receive a physical at camp. Children of staff members must have their medical forms completed before arrival at camp.
  4. The employee is covered under a Workmen's Compensation Insurance Policy, which covers medical expenses for accidents occurring in the course and pursuit of employment. In addition, $30.00 will be deducted from each employee's first week's salary to cover medical insurance (whether or not the employee has a private plan). Any medical expenses, including medications, not covered under Workmen's Compensation Policy shall be borne by the employee and the employer shall have no responsibility for such expenses. All medications needed for pre-existing conditions are the sole responsibility of the employee. They will not be paid for by Shire Village.
  5. The employer reserves the right to withhold $15.00 (or more) or the employee's salary to be used to defray personal expenses believed to have been incurred by the employee. The employer will refund the unused portion of this amount within three (3) months after camp closes.
  6. The employee's laundry may be included with the camper's weekly laundry; otherwise it is the sole responsibility of the employee. If done by camp, camp is not responsible for loss or damage.
  7. If, during the camping season, the employee is unable to perform satisfactorily the services for which they were engaged, the employer at its sole discretion, may dismiss the employee, and the employer shall have no further responsibility to the employee except to pay the employee a pro-rata share of the salary listed in item 15 to date of termination, subject to item 5.
  8. The employer may dismiss the employee at any time during the camping season, without notice, for the employee's failure to live up to the terms and conditions of employment, set by the employer, or for any other good cause as determined by the employer. In the event of a dismissal, the employer shall have no responsibility to the employee, except as indicated in item 7. Any employee dismissed by the employer is not to visit camp for the remainder of the summer and must leave camp within the timeframe determined by the director.
  9. In the event that camp is diminished in enrollment or cannot open due to circumstances beyond the control of the employer, this agreement shall be null and void and of no force and effect. In the event of curtailment of the camp season for any reason whatsoever, the employer will pay the employee a pro-rata share of the salary referred to in item 7 for services performed to the date of curtailment.
  10. The employee grants the employer the right to use the employee's picture in any camp publication or publicity release and the employee hereby grants the employer the right to quote from any written report or material written by the employee for camp use.
  11. Shire Village Camp is run on a non-competitive, inclusive, and humanitarian basis. The employer interprets this to mean that the use of threats, shame, or physical violence towards any person at camp would be contrary to these principles. We create a welcoming, respectful, inclusive, and supportive community for all campers, families, and staff. With these ideas in mind, we are committed to providing a safe and caring community for transgender families, campers, and staff. The employee agrees to adhere to this philosophy and abide by it, and to try to guide campers at all times with patience, kindness, and fairness. Staff will attend training sessions related to camp philosophy during the summer.
  12. The workweek will be 7 consecutive days. The day off starts at 9:30 p.m. and ends two calendar days later at 7:30 a.m. Each staff member will receive the following days off:
    1. 8-week employees will have 7 days off, which may include half days off.
    2. 5-week employees will have 4 days off, which may include half days off.
  13. Employees may have visitors but for no more than two consecutive days (one night) per session. Furthermore, it is the responsibility of the employee to see that any visitors sign in at the office upon their arrival at camp and adhere to visitor's rules during their stay in camp. No staff visitors may stay overnight in any camp building.
  14. Shire Village is not responsible for employee property that is lost, stolen, or damaged.
  15. The employee further agrees to the following work arrangements and regulations:
    1. The employee has read the job description relating to their position and will perform the duties described therein to the best of the employee's ability.
    2. The employee agrees that they are willing to accept a different job assignment should the camp situation require it and the assignment lies within the range of the employee's abilities.
    3. The employee will comply with the requirements of the employer concerning reports. It is understood that the employee will prepare and submit reports on required forms, if any, in the area in which they work, such reports to be handed in before receiving salary.
    4. The employee will attend a camp "get together" to be held in June, where parents and children have the opportunity to meet staff.
    5. All staff, excluding kitchen and riding staff and the nurse, will be assigned to be O.D. (on duty) from 9:30 p.m. until 12:00 a.m. not more than an average of one night per week.
    6. All staff will participate in Camp Council meetings, being required to attend at least 1 and no more than 3 meetings during the summer.
    7. Smoking is not permitted by anyone on camp property, except in the designated smoking area. Smoking is never permitted while any employee is leading or participating in an activity or when in close proximity to children. Smoking is only permitted during an employee's scheduled time off. It is also understood that camp trips, with or without campers, are considered to be an extension of camp and that the aforementioned rules apply.
    8. If the employee is a bunk counselor, the employee and their co-counselor will arrange for one of them to be in the cabin by 12:00 a.m. when the OD (on duty) is through with patrol, and will remain there all night.
    9. Alcoholic beverages, other drugs and/or hallucinogens or other consciousness altering substances are not to be brought to, kept on, or used at camp, whether by campers, staff, or staff guests. Staff is not to be under the influence of any mind-altering substance while working with campers. It is further understood that staff will respect national and state laws concerning the use of controlled substances. It is also understood that camp trips, with or without campers, are considered to be an extension of camp and that the aforementioned rules apply.
    10. Technology at Shire Village Camp: SVC attempts to create a technology free environment for our campers. As a result, individual devices (cell phones, lap tops, tablets, etc) are not used in the presence of campers. Designated areas with access to wifi will be made available during employee time off. Use of technology during program must be reviewed and approved by the directors.
    11. There will be one June orientation meeting in New York City before the camp season begins.
    12. All employees are required to attend a weekly full staff meeting and at least one weekly meeting with a supervisor.
    13. The following pertain to our campers:
      1. Any child exhibiting difficulty in their living at camp should be brought to the attention of the Group Counselor, Assistant Director, or Director.
      2. Campers should wash before each meal and should shower and shampoo every other day, change underclothes daily, and dress appropriately to the weather. All staff is directly concerned with the physical and mental welfare of each camper.
      3. Swimming instruction is required of each camper. Staff must support and enforce this policy.
      4. There must be staff coverage at all times if one or more campers are in the cabin, or any building. No co-ed campers are permitted in cabins unless a special program has been planned and approved.
      5. All food in camp is provided from the kitchen. Food of any kind is not permitted in cabins.
      6. Gum and candy are not allowed at camp.
      7. There is a definite time for lights out for each group. Children may read quietly in bed, using flashlights for a period of time determined by their counselors, but they may not disturb other children at that time.
      8. Campers may not keep money in the bunk. A small amount may be left in the office for the purchase of necessary personal items.
      9. Blow dryers, electronic blankets, and electronic games are not permitted in camp.
      10. There are limits placed on the playing of amplified music in camp. These limitations may pertain to time, place, and/or volume.
  16. Position: 2017 > Title, Salary: $2017 > Base salary + Longevity Bonus: $2017 Longevity Bonus
  17. Employees are expected to fulfill their contracts. However, if an employee must leave before the completion of their contract, this will result in a pro-rated loss of salary. Unscheduled early departure may negatively affect the employee's right to future employment by Shire Village.

We welcome you to our staff and look forward to a rewarding work experience through our mutual efforts and cooperation.
